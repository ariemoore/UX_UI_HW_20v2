Nonprofit Website Redesign

Rape Recovery Center / raperecoverycenter.org


UX/UI Designers:
Arielle Moore, Jenny Clausen & Kaitlyn Clarke

Overview


The Rape Recovery Center (RRC) is a non-profit center in Salt Lake City that provides help and resources for victims of rape and sexual abuse.

The RRC provides:


    24-hour crisis line
    Support for survivors and their families
    Resources and education for both survivors, their loved ones, and volunteers

Problem Statement


Survivors coming to the RRC's website need to quickly and easily find the help that they need. Volunteers need to be able to find resources and clear instructions for how they can help support survivors and become an advocate for others.


How might we make the RRC website more clear and simple, and make their important information as accessible as possible?

Stakeholder Interview

We were able to conduct an interview with the Outreach Coordinator of the RRC

Key insights:

1. Desire for a collective brand and vision

2. Use calming colors and photos to avoid triggering survivors

3. Site must have crisis line and quick exit button

4. Need for use of pseudonym to avoid using company name and logo without consent

Dec 2020

RRC Website Tesing


Objective: to test navigation and identify any pain points

Users:


    1. Disliked excessive wording
    2. Fairly easily navigated through website
    3. Wanted a dropdown menu for "Our Services"
    4. Wanted most important things on website to be on the homepage
    5. Were unclear of purpose of website


Mood Boards

5-second testing

User Impressions


Didn't know what the site was for -
many guesses "women's empowerment"

Design did appear trustworthy

Clean and organized design

5-seconds was a very short amount of time

Usability testing / 7 users

Users:


Wanted a success/completion page after the donation page

Liked the hover effects on the nav bar

Were distracted by the bolding hover effects,
didn't like those

Found clickability issues

Wanted an email, text, or chat option to connect with
RRC without having to call the crisis line for help

User Quote

Anytime I'm feeling super vulnerable, talking to somebody is usually the last thing I want to do.

Anonymous

Moving forward

Add a chat or text feature so people don't have to only call the crisis line to talk to someone

Have the menu and submenu's fixed when you're scrolling so you can access that information throughout



There was an emotional connection to the photos and donations page - photos and icons were very powerful tools for users

Users want more important imformation on the homepage, and clearer information on how to support a survivor or become an advocate
